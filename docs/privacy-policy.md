# Jellyfin Music Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 25, 2026

Jellyfin Music is a music player for connecting to a Jellyfin server that you choose. The app does not operate a developer-owned music service, analytics backend, advertising network, or tracking service.

## Information the App Uses

The app may store the following information on your device:

- Jellyfin server URL, username, user ID, and access token after you sign in.
- Music library metadata returned by your Jellyfin server, such as track titles, artists, albums, durations, genres, and artwork URLs.
- Playback state, queue state, favorites, settings, widget state, and offline/cache metadata.
- Album artwork and other cache files used to make browsing and playback faster.
- Optional Google Play purchase state for the one-time support purchase.

## How Information Is Sent

The app sends requests to the Jellyfin server URL that you enter so it can sign in, browse your library, stream audio, load artwork, update playback progress, and manage app features tied to your Jellyfin account. If you use an `https://` Jellyfin URL, that traffic is encrypted in transit by HTTPS. If you choose an `http://` server URL, that traffic is not encrypted by the app.

Google Play Billing is used only for the optional one-time support purchase. Purchase processing is handled by Google Play.

## What the Developer Receives

The app does not send your Jellyfin login, token, server URL, music library, listening history, or app settings to the app developer.

## Data Sharing

The app does not sell data and does not share your Jellyfin data with advertising or analytics services. Data is sent only to:

- The Jellyfin server you choose to connect to.
- Google Play, if you use the optional support purchase.

## Your Controls

You can sign out from the app to remove the saved Jellyfin session. You can also clear app storage from Android system settings to remove local settings, cache, artwork, playback state, and saved library metadata.

## Contact

For privacy questions, use the support contact listed on the app's Play Store listing or project repository.
